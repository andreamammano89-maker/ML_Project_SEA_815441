# AI in Business: Beyond Efficiency

**Author:** Matteo Cellini
**Context:** This document outlines an experiment to observe how business and thinking change when AI is introduced into operations. 

## 1. Company Background: Alkemy-Retex Group
* **Scale:** The company has approximately 2,150 employees and generates €225M in revenue.
* **Core Business:** The company focuses on business evolution through data, technology, and creativity. Expertise includes Customer Engagement, Business Consulting, Data & AI, Retail Solutions, Technology, and Design.

## 2. The Core Problem: The AI Productivity Paradox
AI acts as a **multiplier**, amplifying what already exists in the system. 

* **The Visible Results:** Production and capacity go up, while the time required goes down. Models run and output grows.
* **The Hidden Tensions:** Quality becomes unstable, and rework increases. 
* **The Paradox:** "We produce more in less time... but we don't earn more". Value does not grow, and margins remain flat. 

This is an issue of efficiency versus value—a business problem, not just a technical one. It is driven by outdated hourly pricing models (which no longer reflect value produced) and efficiency being eroded by unplanned corrections.

## 3. Your Mission: Project 1 - AI Productivity
**Objective:** Analyze what truly happens to margins when AI enters operational processes. You must understand the systemic mechanisms of AI, not just measure its output.

### The Dataset
You will be working with a real-world dataset that is multi-table, incomplete, and imperfect. 
* **Unit of Analysis:** The single task / deliverable.
* **Included Variables:** Hours & SLA, Quality & Rework, Revenue & Cost, AI Usage.

### Key Analytical Steps
To truly understand the system, you must be able to explain *why*, *when*, and *for whom* things happen.
1. **Where is value created?** Identify the specific tasks where AI produces a measurable and positive impact.
2. **Where are losses incurred?** Track the hidden costs, rework, and declining quality.
3. **AI -> quality or just speed?** Distinguish between these two effects in the data.
4. **When does it become negative?** Find the critical threshold beyond which AI usage harms the operating margin (e.g., "Beyond X% of AI usage, rework eliminates margin").
5. **Identify Missing Data:** Determine what other variables or metrics you would need to truly understand the situation.

## 4. Methodology & Evaluation
This project aims to demonstrate that AI is an "augmentation" game (seeing further and connecting new ideas), not just an "efficiency" game (going faster).

### Using AI
* You are explicitly required to use AI for this project.
* **Crucial Rule:** The final answer is not evaluated; **how you used AI to get there is evaluated**. 

### Deliverables Required
Your final presentation/output must include:
* 3 key insights.
* 1 concrete decision.
* 1 thing discovered thanks to AI.
* 1 mistake made by AI.
* The complete log of questions asked and prompts used.

### Evaluation Criteria (What "Good" Looks Like)
Evaluators will look at the depth of your analysis, the quality of questions you asked, your prompt iterations, and your ability to critique the AI. 

A successful project will:
* Find a concrete **mechanism**, not just isolated data points.
* Connect at least **3 variables**.
* Lead to a **clear business decision**.
* Show exactly where AI provided real help and new insights.
